Privacy Policy
Document version: 1.1
Last updated: July 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to DubbiOvi, an open-access AI-assisted audiovisual translation platform developed at the University of Oviedo (Spain) for teaching, research, and knowledge transfer.

DubbiOvi has been designed according to the principles of Privacy by Design and Privacy by Default (Article 25 of the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 – GDPR). Its local-first architecture minimises the collection, processing, and storage of personal data by ensuring that users' audiovisual materials remain on their own devices unless they explicitly choose to use external services.

This Privacy Policy explains:

what personal information may be collected;
why it is collected;
how it is processed and protected;
how long it is retained;
and the rights available to users under applicable data protection legislation.

By downloading or using DubbiOvi, you acknowledge the terms described in this Privacy Policy.

2. Data Controller

Administrative registration data associated with the download of DubbiOvi are processed under the responsibility of the University of Oviedo, within the framework of the DubbiOvi project.

The Project Lead coordinates the day-to-day administration of these data exclusively for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy.

Processing activities are carried out in accordance with the principles established by the GDPR, the Spanish Organic Law 3/2018 on the Protection of Personal Data and Guarantee of Digital Rights (LOPDGDD), and the University of Oviedo's internal data protection regulations where applicable.

3. Personal Data We Collect

When users voluntarily register to download DubbiOvi, the following information may be collected:

First name
Last name
Email address
Institution or organisation
Country
Operating system (Windows or macOS)
Downloaded software version
Date and time of registration

No payment information, banking details, or government-issued identification documents are requested or stored.

Only the minimum amount of information necessary for project administration and communication is collected, following the principle of data minimisation established by the GDPR.

4. Data We Do Not Collect

DubbiOvi follows a local-first computing model.

The application does not automatically collect, upload, monitor, analyse, or transmit:

audiovisual files;
videos;
audio recordings;
subtitles;
scripts;
translations;
terminology databases;
translation memories;
exported projects;
documents;
usage statistics;
behavioural analytics;
telemetry;
keystrokes;
screen recordings.

Users retain full control over their work, which remains stored locally on their own computers unless they intentionally choose to use external AI services.

5. Use of External Artificial Intelligence Services

Some optional DubbiOvi features allow users to interact with third-party Artificial Intelligence providers (for example, Google Gemini).

When these services are used:

only the information strictly necessary to process the user's request is transmitted;
processing is governed by the privacy policy and terms of the selected provider;
the University of Oviedo and the DubbiOvi project do not intercept, monitor, archive, or permanently store the information exchanged with those services.

Users remain responsible for ensuring that the use of external AI services complies with the data protection requirements established by their institutions and by any applicable legislation.

6. Purpose and Legal Basis of Processing

Registration data are processed exclusively for legitimate academic and project-management purposes, including:

managing software downloads;
understanding the dissemination and academic impact of DubbiOvi;
communicating software updates;
notifying users of significant security issues;
producing fully anonymised and aggregated statistics for research, reporting, and knowledge-transfer activities;
improving future versions of the software.

Personal data are never processed for commercial advertising, profiling, or marketing purposes.

Where applicable, processing is based on the user's voluntary provision of registration information and on the legitimate interests associated with the development, maintenance, dissemination, and scientific evaluation of the DubbiOvi project.

7. Data Storage and Security

Administrative registration data are stored using secure institutional Google Workspace services managed by the project administrator.

Access is limited exclusively to authorised project personnel acting within the scope of their responsibilities and subject to confidentiality obligations.

Reasonable technical and organisational safeguards are implemented, including, where applicable:

authenticated access;
encrypted communications (HTTPS);
password-protected institutional accounts;
regular software updates;
secure version control;
periodic backups of administrative records where appropriate.

These measures are intended to preserve the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the information under the responsibility of the project.

8. Data Sharing

Personal information is never sold, rented, licensed, or disclosed to third parties for commercial purposes.

Only fully anonymised and aggregated statistical information (for example, downloads by country or institution type) may be included in:

scientific publications;
conference presentations;
institutional reports;
funding applications;
impact assessments;
knowledge-transfer activities.

No individual user will be identifiable through these statistics.

9. Data Retention

Registration data are retained only for as long as necessary to support the DubbiOvi project, communicate relevant software updates, fulfil legitimate academic purposes, and comply with applicable legal obligations.

When personal information is no longer required, it will be securely deleted or irreversibly anonymised where appropriate.

Users may request deletion of their personal information at any time.

10. Your Rights

Subject to the applicable provisions of the GDPR and other relevant legislation, users may have the right to:

access their personal information;
rectify inaccurate or incomplete information;
request the erasure of their personal data;
request restriction of processing;
object to processing where applicable;
withdraw consent where consent constitutes the legal basis of processing.

Requests will be handled in accordance with the applicable legal requirements.
